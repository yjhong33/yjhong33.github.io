Ion-Exchange-Driven Phase Engineering: From Experimental Rules Toward Synthesis Prediction

Mapping chemical transformation pathways in metastable nanomaterials — bridging fundamental phase–structure relationships with scalable device applications.

I am an inorganic chemist and nanoscientist working at the interface of nanocrystal synthesis, chemical transformation, and structural characterization. My research uses metastable templates — especially Cu₂₋ₓS — to discover and control new phases through cation and anion exchange.

Guided by crystallographic design principles, including anion-sublattice preservation, lattice-mismatch tolerance, and HSAB concepts, I investigate how phase, structure, and morphology evolve across compositional spaces.

More broadly, I aim to translate these experimental insights into predictive synthesis rules for functional nanomaterials and scalable optoelectronic platforms.

Currently, I work at Samsung Display as a Staff Research Engineer, optimizing InP-based quantum dots for QD-OLED applications and connecting materials chemistry with industrial manufacturing.
