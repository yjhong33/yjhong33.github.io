Building material libraries through phase–structure engineering, one crystal at a time.

I am an inorganic chemist and nanoscientist with deep expertise in the synthesis, chemical transformation, and structural characterization of functional nanomaterials. My research centers on using metastable templates — particularly Cu₂₋ₓS — for phase-controlled discovery of new nanomaterials via cation/anion exchange routes.

With crystallographic design rules (anion-sublattice preservation, lattice-mismatch tolerance, HSAB principles) as the underlying framework, I map the relationships between phase, structure, and function across compositional, morphological, and structural spaces.

Currently, I work at Samsung Display as a Staff Research Engineer, optimizing InP-based quantum dots for QD-OLED applications — bridging material chemistry and manufacturing at industrial scale.

02 — Research Focus

Research Areas

Ion-Exchange Chemistry

Phase-controlled synthesis of chalcogenide nanomaterials via cation/anion exchange using metastable Cu₂₋ₓS templates. Systematic exploration of compositional and structural spaces.

Cation Exchange Anion Exchange HSAB Theory Metastable Phases

Phase & Morphology Engineering

Designing and building experimental nanomaterial libraries across phase, morphology, and composition spaces. Mapping transformation pathways during chemical and thermal conversion.

Phase Engineering Crystal Structure In-situ PXRD Nanoframes

Electrocatalysis

Metal phosphides, chalcogenides, and heterostructure catalysts for HER, OER, ORR, and NRR. Sub-nanometer structure–activity analysis and AEMWE device integration.

HER / OER Water Splitting AEMWE Heterostructures

Optoelectronics & QD-OLED

Perovskite nanocrystals for deep-blue LEDs, InP quantum dot optimization for display applications, and inkjet printing process development for QD-OLED manufacturing.
